 The analysis and implementation of the Heart Disease dataset provided meaningful insights into the relationship between various health indicators and cardiovascular risk. By utilizing structured data analysis techniques, the project helped overcome isolated data interpretation and enabled a comprehensive understanding of patient health conditions The study combined exploratory data analysis with predictive modeling to evaluate the impact of attributes such as age, cholesterol levels, blood pressure, and sleep duration. Visualizations, including scatter plots and regression models, revealed that certain variables—such as sleep hours and cholesterol—show little to no direct correlation, indicating that heart disease is influenced by multiple interacting factors rather than a single parameter. Additionally, machine learning techniques such as Linear Regression were applied to identify patterns and trends within the dataset.
